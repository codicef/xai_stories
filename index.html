Instructions

  • Each session presents 10 patient cases (~20–30 minutes).
  • For each case you will see the patient's clinical evidence (predicted survival, SHAP values) on the left.
  • You will rate four narrative texts (Text A–D), each generated by a different model — you will not see the model name.
  • Rate each text on four criteria using a 1–5 Likert scale, then choose an overall preferred text.
  • Your ratings are saved automatically. Download your data at the end of each session.
  • Do not discuss cases with other evaluators during scoring.

Evaluation criteria (1 = poor · 5 = excellent)

  1. Faithfulness to the Evidence — does the narrative correctly reflect the SHAP values and their directions?
  2. Clinical Plausibility — is the explanation medically reasonable and consistent with clinical knowledge?
  3. Clarity and Communication Quality — is the narrative well-organised, readable, and appropriately concise?
  4. Practical Clinical Usefulness — would this explanation help a clinician understand the model's prediction?
